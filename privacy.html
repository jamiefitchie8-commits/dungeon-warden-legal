Dungeon Warden Privacy Policy

Last updated: 31 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how The Sherbet Lemon, based in Scotland, handles information when you use the Dungeon Warden Alexa skill, a voice-first fantasy dungeon role-playing game set beneath Bellhollow.

Dungeon Warden is provided through Amazon Alexa. Amazon separately handles Alexa voice recordings, transcripts, account information, device information, and Alexa app settings under Amazon's own privacy notices and account controls.

1. Who We Are

Dungeon Warden is operated by The Sherbet Lemon, Scotland.

For feedback and support, you can use the GitHub form linked from this page. GitHub may require you to sign in or create a GitHub account to submit the form: Dungeon Warden feedback and support form. You can also report a gameplay bug inside the Alexa skill: first say "report bug" to hear the privacy warning, then say "report bug" followed by a short description of what went wrong.

Please do not include sensitive personal information in feedback or support messages.

2. What Dungeon Warden Does

Dungeon Warden lets you create a fantasy character, explore Bellhollow and the dungeon below, fight fictional monsters, collect fictional items and in-game gold, and save your progress between sessions.

The game includes fantasy combat, fictional monsters, fictional tavern drinks, and dice-style tavern mechanics using fictional in-game gold only. There are no real-money gambling prizes, cash-out mechanics, account-linking payments, advertisements, or real-money purchases in the current version.

3. Information We Receive or Store

When you use Dungeon Warden, we may receive or store the following information:

  • Alexa request information needed to run the skill, such as an Amazon-provided Alexa user identifier, locale, request type, intent, slots, session identifiers, and device capability information.
  • Game save information, such as your character name, stats, health, inventory, equipment, in-game gold, dungeon location, combat state, progress flags, chests, fountains, quests, and timestamps.
  • Technical information needed to operate, debug, secure, and improve the skill, such as error logs and service logs.
  • Feedback you choose to send through the GitHub feedback form, such as bug reports, suggestions, comments, your GitHub username, and any details you include in your message.
  • Bug reports you choose to send inside the Alexa skill, including the words Alexa transcribes for your report, the skill locale, a hashed Alexa user identifier, a hashed session/request reference, and limited gameplay context such as current game phase, room, town location, player level, health, gold, and enemy type.

We do not currently ask Alexa for your postal address, precise location, phone number, email address, payment details, health information, or account-linking credentials.

Dungeon Warden does not need to receive or store your raw voice recording to run the game. Amazon handles Alexa voice recordings and voice history separately.

4. Feedback

We want to gather user feedback to improve Dungeon Warden. Feedback is optional.

If you send feedback through GitHub, GitHub may require sign-in and may process your username, issue content, device information, and related account data under GitHub's own terms and privacy notices. GitHub issues may be public. Do not post private or sensitive information.

If you report a bug inside the Alexa skill, Dungeon Warden stores the report text and limited gameplay context separately from your game save. This helps us understand what went wrong without storing your full save file inside the report.

We may use feedback to respond to you, fix bugs, improve gameplay and accessibility, tune voice prompts, improve command handling, balance the game, and plan future features.

Unless you ask us not to, you allow us to use feedback, suggestions, ideas, bug reports, and gameplay comments without payment to you for the purpose of improving, developing, marketing, and operating Dungeon Warden. This does not give us ownership of your personal information.

5. How We Use Information

We use information to provide the skill, respond to your commands, save and resume your game, maintain game state, troubleshoot bugs, protect the skill from misuse or technical failure, improve the Alexa experience, respond to feedback, and comply with legal obligations.

Where UK GDPR, EU GDPR, or similar laws apply, our legal bases may include performance of a contract, legitimate interests, consent for optional feedback where required, and legal obligation.

6. Sharing Information

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your information for third-party advertising.

We may share or process information with Amazon Alexa and Amazon Web Services, GitHub for feedback issues, service providers that help us host, store, secure, monitor, or support the skill, professional advisers where needed, and authorities or other parties where required by law or necessary to protect rights, safety, security, or prevent abuse.

7. Storage, Security, and Retention

Dungeon Warden currently stores game saves using Amazon Web Services. Game save data is associated with the Alexa user identifier supplied by Amazon so each player's save can be kept separate.

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect information, including limiting the information we collect and using platform security controls. No online service is perfectly secure.

We retain game saves for up to 24 months after last use, unless you request deletion earlier or a longer period is needed for security, legal, or operational reasons. We retain feedback, support messages, and in-skill bug reports for up to 24 months after final response, resolution, or submission. Operational logs are normally retained for up to 90 days, unless needed longer for security, debugging, abuse prevention, or legal reasons.

8. Your Choices and Rights

You can stop using Dungeon Warden at any time. Depending on where you live, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability, or withdrawal of consent where consent applies.

To request deletion of your Dungeon Warden save or feedback records, use the feedback and support form and choose the privacy/deletion option. This route currently uses GitHub and may require a GitHub account. Do not include sensitive information in the issue. We may need a safe way to identify the correct save before deletion.

Alexa voice history, recordings, and Amazon account-level privacy controls are managed by Amazon. You can review those controls in the Alexa app or your Amazon account privacy settings.

If you are in the UK, you may also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/.

9. Children

Dungeon Warden is not directed to children under 13. The game includes fantasy combat, fictional monsters, fictional tavern drinks, and fictional in-game dice mechanics. Parents and guardians are responsible for deciding whether Dungeon Warden is appropriate for their household.

10. International Transfers

Dungeon Warden may be operated using cloud services that process information in countries other than your own, including the United Kingdom, the United States, and regions where Amazon Web Services, GitHub, and service providers operate. Where required, appropriate safeguards or service-provider commitments are used for international transfers.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. If we make material changes to how we use personal information, we will take reasonable steps to make them clear.
